Japanese Language School Project

I work in support of two professors as they write a book about their longtime research topic, Tacoma's Japanese Language School, and its community. Dr. Hoffman and Dr. Hanneman have interviewed approximately 40 students from the Japanese Language School (which ran for several years until WWII). The interviewees made specific claims to the spaces that they interacted with. I turned the conversations from these interviews into spatial data by way of coding information, georeferencing, and digitizing the places mentioned in interviews. I used ESRI's ArcMap 10.5.1. I discovered a new area of Tacoma occupied by the community that had been previously unknown in the body of work about the Japanese in Tacoma. Publication pending.
