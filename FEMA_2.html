Flooding in Sumner

Flooding is a major concern for cities in the Puyallup River Valley. The melting snow from Mount Rainier flows west through the Puyallup River every year. The river flows through the center of Sumner. The city is largely divided into two halves: north and south. North Sumner is an industrial hub for Franz Bread, Dillanos Coffee, Costco, and REI. South Sumner is a historical neighborhood containing many independent businesses, the biggest YMCA in the state, a Sound Transit train station, and numerous residents.

About Sumner

Sumner is home to about 10,000 people and is located in Pierce County, Washington. Sumner was founded in 1853 and originally named Franklin. The city is known as the Rhubarb pie capital of the world! Sumner's official flower is the daffodil. You can find daffodils painted on city roads. It's a common symbol throughout the town.
