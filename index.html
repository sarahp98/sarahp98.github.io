Graduate School Work

Web GIS

At UWT's Master's of Science in Geospatial Technologies I have learned how to make web maps using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I've learned a little Python, too.

Japanese Language School Project

Tacoma's Pre-WWII Japanese Community

As part of my undergraduate GIS certificate, I completed a project on Tacoma's Japanese Language School. I organized spatial information taken from interviews about students of the JLS. I digitized the locations mentioned in the interviews.

GIS Certificate Work

ArcMap Analysis Skills

I received my GIS Certificate from UWT in the Spring of 2018. I made several posters showing off the skills I learned.

Action Mapping Project

Model Builder and Data Management

I worked for an organization called the Action Mapping Project (AMP) during the summer of 2018. I worked with a small group to build and standardize a GIS workflow to georeference and digitize surveys taken by students in local schools. I built a tool for ArcMap using the software's tool called Model Builder.

Maple Valley GIS Internship

Urban Planning

In the winter and spring of 2018 I completed an internship working for the City of Maple Valley through the University of Washington. I was part of a group of students in which we collaboratively worked to plan the future of Maple Valley's "Legacy" property. Using GIS, I analyzed various aspects of Maple Valley and the property itself.

Port of Tacoma Brownfields

An Analysis for Remediation Opportunities

In the winter of 2018 I participated in a course at the University of Washington where I was assigned to work on an analysis of the brownfields (environmentally contaminated parcels) in the Port of Tacoma.
